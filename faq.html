Chapter 08 — Questions

Honest Answers

The questions everyone actually has before, during, and after starting keto. No hype, no fear-mongering — just what the evidence says.

How long until I'm in ketosis?

For most people, 2–4 days of strict carb restriction (<20g/day) is enough to enter nutritional ketosis. Full fat-adaptation — where your body runs efficiently on ketones and symptoms resolve — takes 2–4 weeks. A 24-hour fast can accelerate this.

Will I lose muscle on keto?

Not if your protein intake is adequate (0.6–1.0g per lb of lean body mass) and you're doing some resistance training. Ketones themselves have a protein-sparing effect. If anything, many people preserve or gain muscle while losing fat.

Is keto safe long-term?

Most human data supports safety for months to a few years with proper monitoring. Get bloodwork every 3–6 months — lipids, kidney function, liver enzymes. Some people thrive long-term; others do better cycling in and out. Consult your doctor if you have kidney, liver, or pancreatic conditions, or if you're pregnant.

Can I drink alcohol?

Dry wine and pure spirits (vodka, whiskey, gin, tequila) have zero or near-zero carbs. Beer, sweet wine, and sugary mixers will kick you out of ketosis immediately. Note: alcohol tolerance drops significantly on keto — drinks hit much harder than you're used to.

How do I test if I'm in ketosis?

Three options: blood ketone meters (most accurate — aim for 0.5–3.0 mmol/L BHB), breath acetone devices (one-time purchase, accurate enough), or urine strips (cheap but only reliable in the first 2–3 weeks before your body reabsorbs ketones).

What's the difference between keto and low-carb?

Low-carb typically means under 100–150g of carbs per day. Keto is much stricter — under 50g, usually under 20–30g net carbs — and specifically aims to induce nutritional ketosis. Low-carb lets you eat some fruit and starchy veg; keto does not.

What about cholesterol?

The typical pattern on keto: triglycerides drop sharply, HDL rises, and LDL can go up, down, or stay the same. Importantly, LDL particle size often shifts from small-dense (harmful) to large-buoyant (less harmful). Ask your doctor for an advanced lipid panel with particle sizing.

Do I need to count calories?

Not strictly, but tracking for the first 2–3 weeks is invaluable for calibrating your intuition. Keto suppresses appetite significantly, so many people naturally eat less. Long-term, most keto dieters eat by hunger — not by spreadsheet.

Can I build muscle on keto?

Yes, though the early weeks may see reduced strength as you fat-adapt. After adaptation, strength returns and hypertrophy is comparable to higher-carb approaches — especially on a "targeted keto" variant with pre-workout carbs for serious lifters.

Will my breath really smell?

Possibly, for the first 1–2 weeks. "Keto breath" is acetone being exhaled. It's temporary — as your body upregulates ketone utilization, less escapes via breath. Sugar-free gum and mouthwash help in the meantime.

Does keto work for women too?

Yes, but there are nuances. Some women do better with 30–50g carbs than 20g, especially around menstruation. Hormonal health matters: don't combine keto with chronic calorie restriction and intense cardio — that combination can disrupt thyroid and menstrual function.

Can I cheat occasionally?

Honestly? Once fat-adapted (after 4+ weeks), an occasional carb meal won't undo everything — you'll bounce back into ketosis within 24–48 hours. But in the first month, consistency matters enormously. Each "cheat" resets the adaptation clock.
