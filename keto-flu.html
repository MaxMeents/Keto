Chapter 06 — The transition

The Keto Flu

Days 2–7 can be rough as your body rewires its fuel system. Nearly all symptoms trace back to one thing: electrolyte depletion. Here's the science — and the protocol that prevents it entirely.

Common symptoms

These appear within the first few days of carb restriction as your body burns through glycogen and loses associated water and minerals.

HeadacheFatigueBrain fog Muscle crampsNauseaIrritability Sugar cravingsPoor sleepDizziness ConstipationWeaknessHeart palpitations
Typically resolves within 3–7 days, though mild symptoms may persist up to 4 weeks during full fat-adaptation.

The fix: electrolytes

Insulin drops cause kidneys to excrete sodium rapidly, pulling other minerals with it. Replacing them resolves symptoms within hours.

  • Na
    Sodium — 3–5g/daySalt your food generously, bone broth, pickle juice, sea salt in water.
  • K
    Potassium — 1–3.5g/dayAvocado, spinach, salmon, mushrooms, coconut water (unsweetened).
  • Mg
    Magnesium — 300–500mg/dayDark chocolate, almonds, pumpkin seeds, or glycinate supplement.
  • H₂O
    Hydration — 3L+/dayWater + electrolytes. Plain water alone dilutes minerals further.
Prevention Protocol

How to skip the flu entirely

Most people who experience keto flu simply underestimated mineral replacement. Do this from day one:

1

Start salt-loading on day 1

Add 1 tsp (6g) of real salt to your water or broth. Spread across the day. Don't wait for symptoms.

2

Supplement magnesium at night

300–400mg magnesium glycinate before bed. Prevents cramps, improves sleep quality during the transition.

3

Front-load potassium foods

A large avocado daily + leafy greens at every meal. Most people's potassium intake on keto is dangerously low otherwise.

4

Eat enough fat

A common mistake: cutting carbs without increasing fat. You'll feel exhausted. Hit 70–75% of calories from fat from day one.

5

Prioritize sleep

8–9 hours. Your body is rebuilding its metabolic infrastructure. Stress and sleep deprivation amplify symptoms.
