Chapter 03 — What to eat

What's on the keto plate

A clear, no-nonsense list of keto-friendly foods and the carb landmines to avoid. Print this. Stick it on your fridge. Shop accordingly.

✓

Eat Freely

  • Meat & poultrybeef, pork, chicken, lamb, bacon, game
  • Fatty fish & seafoodsalmon, sardines, mackerel, trout, tuna, shrimp
  • Eggswhole, pasture-raised preferred
  • Healthy oilsolive, avocado, coconut, MCT
  • Full-fat dairybutter, cream, hard cheeses, sour cream
  • Nuts & seedsalmonds, walnuts, pecans, macadamias, chia, flax
  • Low-carb vegetablesspinach, kale, broccoli, cauliflower, zucchini
  • Avocadounlimited — nearly perfect keto food
  • Berries (small portions)raspberries, blackberries, strawberries
  • Dark chocolate85%+ cacao, small squares
✕

Avoid

  • Grainswheat, rice, oats, corn, quinoa, pasta, bread
  • Sugarsoda, candy, desserts, juice, honey, syrup
  • Starchy vegetablespotatoes, sweet potatoes, yams, parsnips
  • Most fruitbananas, apples, grapes, oranges, tropical
  • Legumesbeans, lentils, chickpeas, peas
  • Low-fat productsusually high in added sugar
  • Refined oilssoybean, corn, canola, margarine
  • Most alcoholbeer, sweet wine, mixers, cocktails
  • Processed "keto" snacksoften hide carbs as sugar alcohols
  • Dried fruitconcentrated sugar — a keto landmine
Sample Day

A full day of keto eating

Here's what a typical, delicious day on keto looks like — hitting all your macros.

Breakfast

Bacon, eggs & avocado

  • 3 eggs scrambled in butter
  • 3 strips thick-cut bacon
  • ½ avocado, sliced
  • Black coffee with heavy cream

Lunch

Salmon & greens bowl

  • 6oz wild salmon, olive oil
  • Mixed greens, cucumber, feta
  • Olive oil + lemon dressing
  • Handful of walnuts
F 55gP 38gC 6g
Snack

Cheese & olives

  • 2oz aged cheddar
  • Handful of kalamata olives
  • Celery sticks + almond butter
F 28gP 10gC 3g
Dinner

Ribeye & cauliflower mash

  • 8oz ribeye in butter
  • Cauliflower mash with cream
  • Sautéed spinach, garlic
  • Square of 90% dark chocolate
